Journeying Beyond Boundaries: Celebrating the Spirit of


Dervla Murphy

About

Dervla Murphy was a pioneering Irish travel writer and adventurer, renowned for her solo journeys across some of the most challenging terrains in the world. Armed with little more than a bicycle, she traveled through remote regions of Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Americas, capturing the essence of the cultures and landscapes she encountered.

Her first and most famous book, Full Tilt: Ireland to India with a Bicycle (1965), chronicled her audacious ride from Ireland to India, establishing her as a fearless traveler and a gifted storyteller. Over the decades, she authored more than 20 travel books, each marked by her keen observations, deep empathy, and unflinching honesty.

Murphy’s work extended beyond travel writing; she was a passionate advocate for social justice, often focusing on the lives of the underprivileged and oppressed in the regions she visited. Her dedication to exploring the world on her terms, and her commitment to writing with integrity and insight, left a lasting impact on the world of travel literature and beyond.

Timeline

  • 1931: Born on November 28 in Lismore, County Waterford, Ireland.
  • 1941: Received her first bicycle at age 10, sparking a lifelong passion for cycling and travel.

“For my tenth birthday my parents gave me a second-hand bicycle and Pappa [her grandfather] sent me a second-hand atlas. Already I was an enthusiastic cyclist, though I had never before owned a bicycle, and soon after my birthday I resolved to cycle to India one day. I have never forgotten the exact spot, on a steep hill near Lismore, where this decision was made. Half-way up I rather proudly looked at my legs, slowly pushing the pedals around, and the thought came ‘If I went on doing this for long enough I could get to India.’” - Dervla Murphy

  • 1963: Embarked on her first major solo journey, cycling from Ireland to India.
  • 1965: Published Full Tilt: Ireland to India with a Bicycle, her debut travel book that gained widespread acclaim.
  • 1966-1980s: Traveled extensively through Ethiopia, Afghanistan, Nepal, Pakistan, Peru, and other remote regions, documenting her experiences.

“The hardships and poverty of my youth had been a good apprenticeship for this form of travel. I had been brought up to understand that material possessions and physical comfort should never be confused with success, achievement and security.” - Dervla Murphy

  • 1983: Published Eight Feet in the Andes, detailing her 1,300-mile trek with her daughter and a mule through the Andes Mountains.
  • 1994: Released The Waiting Land, which focused on her experiences in Nepal.
  • 2001: Published Through the Embers of Chaos, a book about her travels in the Balkans during the aftermath of the Yugoslav Wars.
  • 2005: Published Silverland, which recounted her journey through Siberia in the depths of winter.
  • 2015: Released Between River and Sea: Encounters in Israel and Palestine, a reflective work on her travels in the Middle East.
  • 2022: Passed away on May 22 at the age of 90, leaving behind a legacy of over 20 travel books and countless inspired adventurers.
